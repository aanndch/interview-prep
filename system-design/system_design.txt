CORE PROBLEMS (by relevance to YOUR experience)
------------------------------------------------

LEVEL 1: DIRECTLY MATCH YOUR RESUME (highest priority)

  1.  Design a Real-Time Chat / Messaging System
      → You built P2P texting serving 70%+ clients with 1M+ weekly messages
        (NestJS, RabbitMQ, WebSockets). High relevance.
      Focus: WebSocket management, message ordering, delivery guarantees
      (at-least-once, exactly-once), offline messages, read receipts, typing
      indicators, group chat, end-to-end encryption overview.

  2.  Design a Real-Time Analytics Dashboard
      → You delivered real-time analytics dashboards for 10+ clients
        (React, TypeScript, NestJS). High relevance.
      Focus: Data pipeline (producer → stream processor → OLAP store → API),
      time-series DB (ClickHouse, TimescaleDB), rollups/materialized views,
      WebSocket push to frontend, caching strategies for dashboards.

  3.  Design a Calendar / Scheduling System
      → You built Google/Outlook-like calendar system (Angular, TypeScript).
        High relevance.
      Focus: Recurring event storage (RRULE), conflict detection, timezone
      handling, shared calendars, availability queries, sync protocols.

LEVEL 2: STRONGLY RELEVANT TO YOUR STACK

  4.  Design a Notification System
      → You used Firebase push notifications achieving 30% return-user lift.
      Focus: Multi-channel (push, email, SMS, in-app), template engine,
      delivery retry, rate limiting, user preferences, batching, real-time vs
      batched delivery. RabbitMQ experience transfers directly.

  5.  Design an E-Commerce Platform
      → You built e-commerce apps with Magento/GraphQL for 10+ client stores.
      Focus: Product catalog, inventory management, shopping cart, checkout
      flow, payment processing, order management, idempotency.

  6.  Design a URL Shortener (tinyurl / bit.ly)
      → Classic; covers hashing, key generation, DB scaling, redirect (301 vs
        302), analytics. Tests your ability to design simple, high-throughput
        systems.

   7.  Design a Rate Limiter
       → Mercari & Indeed both ask this. Covers token bucket, sliding window,
         distributed rate limiting (Redis clusters), per-user/per-API-key.

   8.  Design a News Feed / Social Feed (Twitter / Instagram)
       → Tests your understanding of fan-out (push vs pull vs hybrid),
         timeline generation, caching hot feeds. Good for general system design
         fluency.

   9.  Design a Job Recommendation System
       → Indeed asks this. Connects to your analytics dashboard experience.
         Focus: candidate-job matching, indexing, relevance scoring, filtering,
         real-time updates when new jobs/postings appear.

LEVEL 3: CLASSIC PROBLEMS (fill gaps)

   10. Design YouTube / Video Streaming Platform
       → CDN knowledge, video transcoding, adaptive bitrate streaming
         (HLS/DASH), metadata & search.

   11. Design a Distributed Key-Value Store / Cache
       → Covers consistent hashing, replication, quorum reads/writes, gossip
         protocol, hinted handoff. Directly relevant to your Redis/PubSub work.

   12. Design a Marketplace Search & Filtering System
       → Mercari asks this. Covers product search, faceted filtering,

         inventory-aware results.

SYSTEM DESIGN INTERVIEW FORMAT (typical for Japan mid-large)
-------------------------------------------------------------
  45-60 min | Whiteboard / Miro / Google Docs
  Structure:
    1. Clarify requirements (functional + non-functional)  — 5 min
    2. High-level design (components, data flow)            — 10 min
    3. Deep dive (schema, API, bottleneck analysis)         — 20 min
    4. Scale & trade-offs                                   — 10 min

  Japan-specific tips:
    - Mercari & PayPay often ask about microservices & event-driven
    - Indeed asks practical, back-of-envelope calculations
    - Startups focus on pragmatic MVP → scaled design
    - English is fine; explain your reasoning clearly (think out loud)
